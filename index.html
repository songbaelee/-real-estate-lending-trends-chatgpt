Property-sector snapshot

Each sector has a different credit story. Good lending judgment starts with not treating “real estate” as one blob.

Mixed

Multifamily

Long-term demand remains strong, but higher rates, expense growth, and floating-rate debt create stress.

Stressed

Office

Hybrid work, weak leasing, and valuation uncertainty make underwriting more conservative.

Resilient

Industrial

Logistics demand remains supportive, though rent growth and cap-rate assumptions still matter.

Selective

Retail

Grocery-anchored and necessity retail can be attractive. Obsolete retail remains difficult.

Cyclical

Hospitality

Performance depends heavily on market, operator quality, labor costs, and travel demand.

Specialized

Data Centers

Strong demand, but power availability, construction costs, and tenant concentration are key risks.

What loan terms reveal

Loan terms are the market’s body language. LTV, DSCR, spreads, reserves, and recourse show how nervous lenders really are.

Sector Typical LTV DSCR Focus Common Lender Concern Likely Credit Response
Multifamily 55%–70% 1.20x–1.35x+ Rate resets and expense inflation Lower leverage, rate caps, reserves
Office 40%–60% 1.40x+ Vacancy and valuation uncertainty More equity, lower proceeds, leasing reserves
Industrial 55%–65% 1.25x–1.40x Tenant rollover and rent assumptions Tenant diligence, rollover analysis
Retail 50%–65% 1.25x–1.40x Tenant quality and format risk Anchor review, co-tenancy analysis
Hotel 45%–60% Debt yield often key Volatility in cash flow Lower leverage, sponsor strength, reserves

Illustrative ranges only. Replace with current market data before publishing as a live reference.

Major risks facing the sector

This is the credit committee page: what could go wrong, who gets hit, and what indicators deserve a blinking red dot.

High

Refinancing wall

Loans made during the low-rate period may refinance into higher rates, lower valuations, and lower loan proceeds.

High

Office demand reset

Remote and hybrid work continue to pressure occupancy, rents, capital expenditure needs, and collateral values.

Rising

Insurance costs

Premium increases can weaken net operating income, especially in climate-exposed markets.

Rising

Regional bank exposure

Bank pullback can reduce liquidity for borrowers and shift more activity toward non-bank lenders.

Structural

Private credit growth

Private lenders can provide capital when banks retreat, but often at higher cost and with tighter covenants.

Structural

Climate and resilience

Flood, wildfire, heat, and storm exposure increasingly affect underwriting, insurance, repairs, and exit values.

What to watch next

The site should be updated around a few repeatable indicators rather than drowning readers in news confetti.

Fed policy, SOFR, Treasury yields, and mortgage spreads.

CMBS, bank CRE loans, and sector-specific stress signals.

Refinancing volume by year, sector, geography, and lender type.

Cap rates

Insurance

Premium increases, coverage availability, and market-level affordability.

Credit standards

LTVs, DSCRs, spreads, reserves, recourse, and lender appetite.
